Introduction to Residence Time Distribution (RTD)

Ideal Reactors

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the stimulus response technique to study residence time distribution of reactors
  • Visualize the flow pattern in ideal reactors
  • Comprehend the RTD profiles of ideal flow reactors

Simulator Guidelines

  1. Choose simulator parameters: Type of ideal reactor, flow conditions, tracer input
  2. Observe the flow of tracer through the reactor, exit concentration profile and the RTD functions

Self-Assessment

  • How would you conduct a bench scale investigation to study the residence time distribution of flow reactors?
  • What are the key differences / similarities between an ideal PFR and ideal CSTR (flow pattern, RTD profile, RTD parameters)?
  • What are the advantage(s) and disadvantage(s) of pulse and step tracer experiment in studying RTD?

Real Reactors

Learning Objectives

  • Visualize the flow pattern in a real reactor through stimulus response experiment
  • Comprehend flow deviation(s) in the real reactor using its RTD profile

Simulator Guidelines

  1. Choose simulator parameters: Type of real reactor, flow conditions, tracer input
  2. Observe the flow of tracer through the ideal and real reactors and their RTD profiles
  3. Diagnose the real reactor problem through comparison of the RTD profiles of the ideal and real reactor

Self-Assessment

  • How would you conduct a bench scale investigation to study the residence time distribution of flow reactors?
  • What are the key differences in the RTD profiles of the chosen real reactor and its ideal counterpart?
  • What is effect of the non-ideality on the magnitude of RTD parameters?
  • Which non-ideal reactor model will be suitable to predict performance?
