Introduction to Vapour Liquid Equilibrium (VLE):
Binary System

Learning Objectives

  • Visualize phase behaviour (VLE) of a binary system
  • Comprehend the phase behaviour of a binary system
  • Recognize the significant difference between the phase behaviour of a pure component and a binary system

Simulator Guidelines

  1. Choose simulator parameters: Chemical species in binary mixture, initial conditions of the mixture (P, T, composition) and type of phase diagram (P – xy / T – xy / x – y)
  2. Observe the initial state of the mixture and the molecular dynamics at the specified system conditions
  3. For the chosen system, observe how the state of the mixture changes with variations in P/T of the system

Self-Assessment

  • How would you conduct a bench scale investigation to generate the phase diagrams for the chosen binary mixture?
  • How are the P – xy and T – xy plots useful?
  • How is the equilibrium composition plot useful?
  • Why is there a limitation in the system’s initial conditions?
  • What are the key differences between the P – xy and T –xy plots?
  • What are the key differences between the VLE of pure species and binary system?
  • What happens when one of the species in the binary mixture is swapped with a light/heavy hydrocarbon?
